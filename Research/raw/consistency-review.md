# Research Consistency Review

Audit date: 2026-07-21 UTC

Verified code baseline: `fd62b46faf3505d738f6d5800e787473b14cacd6` (`main` and `origin/main` at the start of the audit)

## Review envelope

This review checks the complete research corpus as three different kinds of evidence:

- **QAD** is the decision record. Its canonical answers are in `QAD/decisions.md`; `QAD/provenance.md` maps direct decisions versus user-authorized derivations; trails preserve alternatives, evidence, tradeoffs, assumptions, dependencies, and supersession; specs make accepted behavior precise. A repository-derived recommendation is not silently promoted to a human product preference.
- **CS** records only verified current repository state at the baseline above. It does not describe planned files, branches, services, or rollout behavior as existing.
- **SIM** applies QAD contracts to adversarial and failure-oriented scenarios and compares the required trace with CS. A validated simulation means the decisions determine a coherent expected result, not that the current product implements or passes it.

The audit inspected repository instructions, Git/worktree/ref state, the complete pre-existing `Research/` directory, cited implementation and test files, relevant schemas and service boundaries, and official external compatibility sources. No product code was changed or executed as an implementation.

## Decision-record audit

All 238 defined QAD identifiers are unique and cross-referenced. Every canonical decision has exactly one local `Rationale`. A mechanical trail audit found 185 individual/grouped QAD sections and zero without a nearby explicit `Why`, `Reason`, `Rationale`, `Safety boundary`, `Security/soundness gap`, or `Risk` statement. The recovered task and `QAD/provenance.md` now distinguish direct user decisions, user-authorized derivations, accepted-decision consequences, and repository conclusions instead of treating all reconstructed trail wording as unverified. The decision index, subject indexes, trails, specs, and simulations agree on the controlling authority. Trail audit sections additionally cover credible alternatives and rejection, why the selected behavior follows from evidence, security/operations/compatibility/maintenance/user tradeoffs, assumptions and unknowns, downstream consequences, dependencies, and supersession. QAD-038's earlier unrestricted user-approved answer and the later owner/`Administrator`-only and mandatory-declaration proposals remain superseded provenance. The operative answer preserves Discord-compatible invite mode with the current held-bit ceiling and offers an explicit versioned code-defined mode for the exact-set exception; either committed set becomes the durable relationship ceiling, and later broadening remains owner/`Administrator`-only.

The accepted design is coherent around these central invariants:

- installation authority is a durable, generation-bound relationship rather than inferred bot membership;
- managed-role presentation and lifecycle ownership are determined; invite-mode `Manage Guild` remains limited to held permission bits, code-defined mode permits exactly the saved required set, either committed set becomes the relationship ceiling, and only owner/`Administrator` may later raise it or change hierarchy position/supplemental-role assignments;
- stable IDs/keys and immutable complete manifests own command identity and history, while names and aliases remain presentation;
- current server state is reauthorized at action time; client filtering and snapshots are never the authorization boundary;
- one initial interaction callback is atomically claimed, retries use durable scoped identities, and no exactly-once claim is made without a creation idempotency key;
- ephemeral data has one server-derived recipient and bounded storage/report lifecycles;
- component input is per-user unless a separately authorized shared CAS effect is requested;
- leaving active authority revokes before asynchronous cleanup, and reinstall/recovery creates new generations rather than resurrecting old tokens or claims;
- producer rollback leaves tolerant readers, revocation, cleanup, suspension, expiry, audit, and reconciliation operational.

The hard cross-document quantities were checked for consistent meaning: 3-second initial acknowledgement, 15-minute interaction-token authority, at most five Fluxer follow-ups, 24-hour ordinary ephemeral and handle-release maxima, 5-minute confirmation maxima, 45-day guild-audit and superseded-setting-payload windows, 90-day passive command-use retention, at least 12 months of stable-protocol overlap, report evidence through 180 days after resolution (or 7 days after an authorized hold release), 50 non-deleted applications per owner, 1,000 command identities, newest 20 plus at most 5 pinned manifests, 100 setting definitions, at most 25 lifecycle delivery attempts, and no more than three open dependent pull requests in a train.

## Current-state verification

CS citations were checked against the baseline tree and narrowed to concrete files, symbols, fields, and absences. The important verified gaps are:

- current OAuth installation adds the bot member inline with generic `BOT_INVITE`/human-inviter provenance and creates/assigns an ordinary role only when requested permissions are nonzero; there is no installation aggregate, generation, managed-role owner/role association, lifecycle CAS, or rollback owner;
- current bot-invite and role-update code/tests allow non-owner/non-administrator actors to grant only permissions they already possess and reject a `Manage Guild`-only `Administrator` grant. QAD-038 preserves that behavior in invite mode and intentionally differs only when the application opts into a canonical versioned code-defined set; either path then adds a durable installed ceiling that `Manage Guild` cannot raise later;
- current application creation caps an owner at 25 through a separate list/count then create, so the check is not an atomic capacity claim; the route separately requires a default user, CAPTCHA, and a 10/hour bucket;
- current application command UX is a local native-command list parsed by name; there is no application command registry, manifest history, targeting/alias/preference store, or interaction envelope;
- current user settings provide persistent synchronized account preferences, separate per-community settings, and Gateway updates, but no command hide/favorite/provider-preference/usage schema or inactivity TTL;
- current messages and Gateway have no application-interaction, component-state, recipient-ephemeral, callback-endpoint, signing-key, or transport-activation model;
- current message rows/internal TypeScript/Rust models carry an integer version, but public message request/update/response schemas omit it and current read-then-unconditional-patch updates are not a guaranteed monotonic/CAS boundary;
- current guild audit rows have a 45-day TTL, and the message-delete batching path can delete qualifying source entries and insert one replacement bulk row; the planned application causal chain therefore explicitly excludes its linked rows from destructive compaction;
- current `OutboundEndpoint` rejects unsafe URL structure and a hard-coded subset of private/special literals, while `isPublicIpAddress` has a broader reserved-range table; neither is composed into the DNS-pinned redirect-safe deadline dispatcher required for three-second HTTP interaction delivery;
- bots authenticate and authorize as bot users; current guild audit rows do not represent an application/invoker/interaction/delegated-authority chain;
- current account harvest starts from stored messages authored by the user and exports account/per-community settings; it has no recipient-scoped ephemeral object to include;
- current Gateway rollout configuration already has validated persistence/admin/RPC/NATS propagation and stable-ID percentage precedent, while shared HTTP metrics use bounded labels; neither supplies bot-platform gates, SLOs, or measured observation windows;
- no declarative application-settings store, suspension overlay, report-evidence purge/hold lifecycle, or bot-platform recovery contract exists;
- the repository's versioned update helper is read-increment-upsert behavior, not proof of compare-and-set semantics;
- no Flutter/Dart client exists in the tracked baseline, so the plan requires client-neutral contracts and safe fallback but cannot claim native-client implementation coverage.

These are evidence-backed implementation gaps, not defects silently fixed by the research branch.

QAD-038's authority path is resolved as portable invite mode plus an opt-in code-defined exact-set exception, both bounded by a durable relationship ceiling and later owner/`Administrator` expansion, but this research review is not authorization to implement product code.
